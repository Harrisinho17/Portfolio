Obssesive Compulssive Disorder (OCD) Patient Analysis

Overview

This project will analyze the OCD Patient Dataset, which contains demographic and clinical data from 1,500 individuals diagnosed with Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD). The dataset includes patient demographics, symptom severity (Y-BOCS scores), co-occurring conditions, treatment history, and family history of OCD. The goal of this project is to explore patterns, correlations, and insights related to OCD symptoms, treatment effectiveness, and potential contributing factors. By examining these variables, we aim to answer key research questions about OCD presentation, severity, and treatment outcomes.

Github Repository

Topics to be analyszed

Demographic Trends & OCD Onset

• What is the age distribution of patients diagnosed with OCD?

• Are there significant differences in symptom severity (Y-BOCS scores) across different age groups?

• Does gender influence the type or severity of OCD symptoms?

• Are there notable differences in OCD severity based on marital status or education level?

• How does ethnicity correlate with symptom severity or treatment patterns?

Co-Occurring Mental Health Conditions

• What percentage of OCD patients also have a depression or anxiety diagnosis?

• Do patients with co-occurring depression or anxiety have higher Y-BOCS scores?

• Is there a relationship between previous psychiatric diagnoses and OCD severity?

OCD Symptoms & Severity Analysis

• What are the most common obsession and compulsion types in the dataset?

• Do specific obsession types correlate with higher or lower Y-BOCS scores?

• Are compulsions more severe than obsessions based on Y-BOCS scores?

• Does having a family history of OCD impact symptom severity?

Treatment & Medication Insights

• What are the most commonly prescribed medications for OCD patients?

• Is there a relationship between medication use and symptom severity (Y-BOCS scores)?

• Do patients with longer symptom durations receive different treatments compared to those with shorter durations?

• Are there differences in medication prescriptions based on demographic factors (e.g., age, gender, ethnicity)?
